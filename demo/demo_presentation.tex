\section{An overview of the \texttt{pittsburgh} template}
\label{sec:org326dfab}
\begin{frame}[label={sec:orgb4da410},fragile]{What is the \texttt{pittsburgh} template?}
 \texttt{pittsburgh} is a beamer template based on the widely used \texttt{metropolis} theme.

It's called "pittsburgh" because Pittsburgh, PA is a metropolis.
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}[label={sec:org9a6dcee},fragile]{Why?}
 I \emph{love} the aesthetics of Apple's Keynote presentations on macOS but
\begin{enumerate}
\item Keynote only runs on macOS and thus presentations are lost when switching OSs,
\pause
\item Its files tend to be quite large relative to their content, and
\pause
\item Its math fonts don't match the \LaTeX{} ones.
\pause
\end{enumerate}
Beamer fixes the above and \texttt{pittsburgh} attempts to carry over the slick design of Keynote.
\end{frame}

    For $t= 1,2,...$:

    \quad Agent observes a context $X_t $ (typically in $\mathbb R^d$).

    \quad Agent takes action $A_t \sim h_t(\cdot \mid X_t)$.

    \quad Agent observes reward $R_t$ (typically in $\mathbb R$).

    EndFor

  \end{pghcolorbox}
}
